Best budget ice cream makers

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

For making creamy, homemade ice cream without overspending, the Cuisinart ICE-21 is the reliable workhorse that delivers smooth results every time.

If you want to skip the pre-freezing step entirely, the Breville Smart Scoop is worth the splurge; if counter space is tight, the Dash My Pint churns single servings in minutes.

A good ice cream maker turns cream, sugar, and a little imagination into something far better than anything from the freezer aisle. But you don’t need to drop a lot of money to get consistently creamy results. The trick is matching the machine to how you actually cook: how much freezer space you can spare for the bowl, how often you plan to churn, and whether you prefer a hands-off approach or don’t mind babysitting the mix. Most budget-friendly models use a pre-frozen bowl, you stash it in the freezer for 12–24 hours, then pour in your custard and let the machine do the rest. That means you need to plan ahead, but the payoff is a simpler, cheaper machine that still makes excellent ice cream, sorbet, or gelato. The three picks below cover the sweet spot of value: one that does the job reliably, one that adds convenient automation, and one that won’t crowd your counter.

Our verdict

For most people, the Cuisinart ICE-21 is the sweet spot: it’s affordable, trustworthy, and makes excellent ice cream without unnecessary complexity. And if you’re short on space or just want a pint at a time, the Dash My Pint delivers a charming, low-commitment experience.
